SK Macharia Undergoes Cleansing to Reverse Curse Pronounced on Him by Kikuyu Elders

- The elders kickstarted the event by sanitising their hands in line with the Ministry of Health protocols before proceeding to perform the ceremony that lasted one hour

- The ceremony followed a 2017 curse that was spelled on Macharia by a section of Kikuyu elders for supporting Raila Odinga

- Macharia's association with Raila sparked a revolt from a section of the public, especially from the Mt Kenya region, who vowed to boycott his TV and Radio stations accusing him of betrayal

Members of the Kikuyu Council of Elders have held a ceremony to cleanse Royal Media Services chairman SK Macharia in Ndakaini area of Gatanga constituency in Murangá county.

Led by their chairman Dominic Ngera, the elders under the Kiama Kiama umbrella trooped to the area on the morning of Friday, April 16, for the ceremony.

The elders kickstarted the event by sanitising their hands in line with the Ministry of Health protocols aimed at curbing the spread of COVID-19 before proceeding to perform the ceremony that lasted one hour.

According to the elders, the ceremony followed a 2017 curse that was spelled to Macharia by a section of Kikuyu elders for allegedly supporting Raila Odinga during the year's General Election which they termed as a betray to the community.

"There are some people who did not have good intentions for our community. They came here and performed some rituals alleging that they had cursed this particular place including the residents of Ndakaini as well as SK Macharia," said Ngera.

What many people don't know is that the relationship between Macharia and the Odinga family dates way back to the 90s.

Macharia had previously revealed during a media interview that he supported Raila's father, Jaramogi Oginga Odinga, and even campaigned for him while he faced the late President Daniel Moi during the 1992 Elections.

And according to the elders, theirs was to make sure that they exorcise all the curses that were spelled on Macharia for his actions and the Kikuyu community at large.

"We came here with only one intention to make sacrifices and also pray to God to remove all the curses that were spelled on Macharia and our community," Ngera added.

During the ceremony, the elders made a sacrifice of sheep to sanctify the place used by the separate faction who are said to have spelled a curse on Macharia.

"We have now finished all this issue even those who were involved we have since forgiven them. They cannot come back again and make the curse. Our community is led by respectable elders who execute their duties in a manner meant to bring peace and unity in the community," explained Ngera.

The elders also used the opportunity to pray for the community and the country at large to have peace and unity.

TUKO.co.ke understands that the relationship between Macharia and the Odinga family has continued to blossom since the 90s and perhaps this is a clear indication of why the former rallied behind Raila in the 2013 and 2017 elections as he faced Uhuru Kenyatta.

Macharia was previously appointed part of a CORD summit that was to campaign for the Opposition leader.

His appointment sparked a revolt from a section of the public, especially from the Mt Kenya region, who vowed to boycott his TV and Radio stations accusing him of betrayal.

This, however, did not sway Macharia from supporting the ODM Party leader.
