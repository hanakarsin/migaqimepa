Moi University: Eldoret police allow students to hold demonstration over HELB money delays

- They, however, acknowledged the importance of HELB loan in facilitating student affairs

- The students added that the COVID-19 pandemic effects had made them dependent on the fund since they could not afford upkeep money

Police in Eldoret have granted Moi University students permission to hold demonstrations in protest against Higher Education Loans Board (HELB) disbursement delays.

The students, through Moi University Students Organisation (MUSO) secretary-general Wandia Jean Ryan, sought permission at Eldoret Central Police Station, Turbo sub-county on Monday, March 8.

According to their memo to authorities, the aggrieved students intend to hold a peaceful demonstration across Eldoret Town on Wednesday, March 10.

"The aim of this demonstration is to make the government aware of delayed HELB loan disbursement to specifically Moi University students," read part of the memo.

"Students from other universities especially in Nairobi were awarded their loans after holding demonstrations too,” it read on.

The students accused HELB of sidelining Moi University, and that numerous efforts to reach the parastatal had not borne fruits.

Turbo sub-county police commander approved the request by the students and directed them to ensure the marching from Zion Mall to Central Primary School in Eldoret Town was peaceful and in conformity to COVID-19 protocols.

“Approved so long as the procession is peaceful and COVID-19 protocols are followed,” read the approval by the area police boss.

According to Wandia, they were organising the demonstration to air out their disgruntlement over the discriminatory manner in which HELB had handled their affairs as students in terms of disbursements for school fees and upkeep.

"This follows exploration of all available channels including seeking the attention of the HELB officers including the CEO, all in vain,” stated Wandia.

The MUSO secretary-general, however, acknowledged the importance of HELB loan in facilitating student affairs, adding that the effects of COVID-19 pandemic had made students more dependent on the fund.

"The current circumstances around COVID-19 and increased learning expenses have made the situation more precarious. Some comrades are sleeping hungry and unable to pay rent causing much anxiety ahead of the exams,” added Wandia.

They further demanded that HELB acknowledges and addresses the discrimination, why the university was left out and that it commits to a date on which they will make the disbursements.

Story by Joseph Alovi, TUKO.co.ke campus correspondent, Moi University, main campus, Kesses
