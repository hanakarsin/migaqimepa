Safaricom apologises over tweets asking government to keep off its affairs

A series of anti-government tweets were posted on Safaricom's official Twitter account on the night of Wednesday, July 3, in not so palatable corporate language.

The statements which have since been deleted asked the government to keep off the telco's affairs and concentrate on improving its failed state corporations that are struggling to stay afloat.

Such companies included Kenya Pipeline Corporations, Kenya Airways (KQ), National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF) and ailing sugar millers Muhoroni and Mumias factories.

"Let the government first improve management in Muhoroni, NHIF, Mumias, Kenya Airways and Kenya Pipeline and many other organisations that have deliberately been run down by state appointees before they can think of imposing their choice on Safaricom," tweeted Safaricom.

The author also went on name calling spree while at the same time dragging Deputy President William Ruto's name into the 2022 politics.

In its apology though, the telecommunication giant clarified the unfortunate incident was as a result of a system glitch and was not in any way intentional neither was its position.

"Good morning. We apologise for the series of tweets that went out from our official accounts last night. We had a system glitch that has since been rectified. Thank you," explained Safaricom.

This comes at a time there is a lot of jostling and lobbying for Kenyan to replace CEO Bob Collymore who died on Monday, July 1, after battle with cancer.

The company settled on founding boss Michael Joseph on interim basis.
