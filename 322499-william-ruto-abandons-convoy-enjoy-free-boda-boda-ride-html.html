William Ruto abandons convoy to enjoy free boda boda ride

A boda boda operator in Nakuru became the centre of attention when he offered Deputy President William Ruto a free ride in Salgaa area on Saturday, November 2.

The DP was in the area to launch the rehabilitated Salgaa-Rongai road when opted to ride on the motorcycle much to surprise of area residents who turned up to receive him.

The rider identified as Jafferson Tanui alias Jamaa wa Boda first had a light introductory conversation with the man he usually sees on TV.

And soon his special 'client' jumped on the a two-wheeled machine.

Addressing the area residents, Ruto stressed on the government commitment to improving infrastructure toward realisation of the the Big Four agenda.

Away from Salgaa road, the country's second in command also visited home of Lelechwet village elder who passed on recently and connected his family to electricity.

"Crucially, we are linking every household and business to affordable and reliable power to increase productivity and boost growth,"he added.

The government is targeting to link at least 70% of Kenyan households to national grid under the Last Mile Connectivity Project.
