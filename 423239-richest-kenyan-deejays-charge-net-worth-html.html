Despite deejaying being regarded by some as unworthy, the job is one of the highest paying in the entertainment industry.

Deejays in Kenya have made it a lot easier for artistes to get airplay for their single-song projects.

1. DJ Joe Mfalme

Joseph Mwenda, popularly referred to as DJ Joe Mfalme, runs DJ Joe Mfalme Ltd, offering top-notch deejay services. The entertainer has, over the years, been able to acquire a large following that is growing daily.

According to his website, he was born in December 1987 in Meru. His interest in being a DJ begun at the early age of 13 while listening to some of Kenya's pioneer deejays.

He had a particular liking for DJ Pinye, who was his inspiration. He later competed for Pilsner Mfalme DJ Competition 2008, where he became the 1st runner-up.

In 2010, he won the coveted prize, and that’s where he got his second name Mfalme meaning King.

Capital FM thereafter hired him. He has since established himself as Dj who can play for various audiences, age groups, and demographics.

He also offers various services, including event deejay services, event audio solutions, social media influencers, and most recently, brand activations and brand partnerships.

He charges KSh 170,000 for regional events and over KSh 210,000 for international events.

For club gigs, one would part with KSh 40,000 to KSh 50,000, while special occasions cost a minimum of KSh 50,000.

Hiring him for party and university shows cost between KSh 60,000 to KSh 70,000 according to The Standard.

According to Kenya Cradle, his networth is estimated to be between $500,000 (KSh 54.8 million) and $1 million (KSh KSh 109.6 million).

2. DJ Mo

Samuel Muraya, known for his stage name DJ Mo is one of the best gospel DJs in Kenya. He scooped the 2013 Groove Award Deejay of the Year.

The 34-year-old is married to popular gospel musician Size 8 (Linet Masiro Munyali).

The entertainer charges KSh 140,000 and KSh 190, 000 for regional and international events and between KSh 100,000 to KSh 50, 000 for corporate events.

In 2019, DJ Mo and Size 8 moved to a new multimillion bungalow in Lavington, Nairobi. Houses in the area cost an arm and a leg, with one paying between KSh 50 million to KSh 100 million to acquire one.

In October 2020, however, he hit the headlines for the wrong reasons after he was accused of cheating on his wife with a lady identified as Margaret Wanyama.

As a result, he lost his job at NTV besides other multimillion deals.

In July, he revealed proceeds from his entertainment gigs make up a small percentage of his total wealth.

“I have always been a businessman. The music industry is my passion and whatever I get from it is very little. 80 percent of whatever I have is from business,” DJ Mo said.

3. DJ Kalonje

DJ Kalonje's music mix has dominated the Kenyan matatu industry for years.

The deejay, whose real name is George Waweru, charges between KSh 250,000 to KSh 350,000 for corporate events.

For campus parties, he has a quotation of between KSh100,000 to KSh 150,000, while club gigs within the capital earn him between KSh 50,000 to KSh 70,000.

4. Kriss Darlin

Nicknamed the 'president DJ' for hosting big state events, Kriss Darlin is popular among reggae lovers.

Kris is the founder of Dohty Family, a group of deejays who major in reggae music.

He was raised in Kibera, but his career as a top-notch DJ afforded him the privilege to choose where he lives.

He has since been on many international tours and has won many awards including Golden Mic Award and Ghetto Stars Award.

The deejay charges between KSh 150,000 to KSh 300,000 for major gigs and approximately KSh 90,000 for club events.

5. DJ Pierra Makena

Makena has beaten the odds in the male-dominated field to emerge as one of the most sought deejays in Kenya.

She studied mass communication but has since settled for deejaying. The versatile DJ also earns money from modeling, acting, and singing.

DJ Pierra Makena charges KSh 120,000 and KSh 180,000 for local and international events and, respectively and KSh 30,000 for club gigs. She, however, said the quotations depend on the client.

On July 31, she counted losses after her Park and Chill event, in Meru, was cancelled at the last minute due to the government's COVID-19 protocols.

Her networth is estimated at between KSh 100 million to KSh 500 million.
