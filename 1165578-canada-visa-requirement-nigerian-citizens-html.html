Medical tests are not necessary if you plan to stay in Canada for six months or less unless you have a job that requires you to come into contact with people or you are an agricultural worker from specific countries.

If you plan to stay for more than six months, you must undergo medical tests if you live in specific countries, intend to do a public health job, or are applying for a parent and grandparent super visa.

Your own doctor cannot do the medical exam. Instead, the relevant authorities give you a list of panel physicians to choose from and instructions on how to get it done. Note that medical test fees are not refundable.

How long does it take to get a Canadian visa in Nigeria?

If you are a Nigerian applying for a Canada visitor visa, you should know the visa processing time varies from 23 days to eight months. Student visas take an average of four months to be approved.

Immigration to Canada is not a walk in the park because there are various steps you must take to successfully apply and get approved for Canadian immigration. Each person may have a different experience, making it challenging to pick one type of visa that is easier to acquire.

Is an interview required for acquiring a Canadian visa?

A personal interview is not always required to acquire a Canadian visa. The decision to have or not have an interview is made by the relevant officials.
