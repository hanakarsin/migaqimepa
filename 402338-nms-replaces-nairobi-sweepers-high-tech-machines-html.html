NMS replaces Nairobi sweepers with high-tech machines

- Though the high-tech machines will reduce human labour, all the affected individuals will be deployed elsewhere

- The NMS said major cities around the world were using the road sweepers to reduce reliance on human labour

- It said the road sweepers will reduce the risk of exposure to the labour force during the COVID-19 pandemic and beyond

The Nairobi Metropolitan Services (NMS) has unveiled three new high-tech Mechanical Road Sweepers that will replace human labour in cleaning the city.

The three new machines will complement three smaller capacity scarab street sweepers which were among the vehicles rehabilitated in the first phase.

A statement from NMS indicated major cities around the world were using the road sweepers to reduce reliance on human labour.

"These will be deployed every night to clean up the city streets replacing the labour intensive system with those individuals to be deployed elsewhere," the statement indicated in part.

Features of the machines

The equipment has a sweeping width of up to 2500 mm, 500litres water storage tank, a suction system to collect debris to a volume of 6 cubic metres and a watering system to reduce the adverse effects of dust operators and the public.

These particular features, the Mohammed Badi-led team added, will reduce the risk of exposure to the labour force during the COVID-19 pandemic and beyond.

"Once the garbage will eventually be carted away to the Dandora Dumpsite," the statement added.

The NMS, however, said the human labour affected by the deployment of the machines will be stationed elsewhere.

Wi-Fi and streetlights

Besides the machines, the NMS recently also announced it will instal free Wi-Fi in the city as part of its development plans.

Additionally, all green spaces and parks in Nairobi will soon have streetlights integrated with free Wi-Fi for use by residents who frequent them.

The new development is part of plans to modernize the smart lighting system for streetlights within Nairobi county.

Michuki Park, City Park, Central and Uhuru parks, Jevanjee Gardens, Jamhuri Park and Ngong Road forest are some of the green spaces that are being targeted.

The shift to the smart light system will also see some of the street lights integrated with protection and security cameras, according to Badi.
