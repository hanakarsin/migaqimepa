Society expects a woman to get married and build a home but this is not the case for reverend Martha Mumbi.

Speaking to Monica Kagoni, the ACK reverend who is her 70s stated three reasons that pushed her into being single.

According to Mumbi, the first reason was the fact that her mom got married at a very young age and she witnessed her struggles after her dad was detained in 1952.

She said that at the time, her mom was pregnant with a third child and was left to take care of them all alone at such a young age.

"I chose to be single. There are three reasons I chose the path. The first reason is my mum's life. She got married at a very young age. My grandmother married her off so that the brother who was following her could go to school... My dad was detained in 1952 and she was left as a young bride. Being the eldest, I needed to help her," narrated Mumbi.

Multiple women

In 1961 when her dad returned from detention, he started involving himself in alcohol and multiple women.

She said that he later remarried, something that affected her mother mentally and she had to be admitted at Mathari Hospital.

"When my dad came back in 1961, he indulged himself in alcohol and would have multiple women. He later remarried, something that affected my mother. The deep relationship between us and him dwindled. My mom became sick and was admitted at MatharI Hospital," added the retired reverend.

According to Mumbi, when her mom got mentally ill, she had to look after their six-month-old baby and at the same time go to school.

Mumbi revealed that after her mom left Mathari Hospital, she gave birth to other kids which made her sick every time she delivered.

Missed secondary school

During her mom's sickness, as the eldest daughter, she was forced to take care of the kids.

"When she came back from Mathari Hospital, she got other babies. Anytime she gave birth, she ended up being sick and I was forced to be their mother. Being a parent to my siblings at such a young age took away my joy," added the reverend.

According to the woman who is in her 70s, she never joined any secondary school after completing her primary education but joined a college where she studied teaching.

"I never went to secondary school. I went to a teaching college. I became a teacher through that. You could not date while in school. It had tough rules. After school, I started taking care of my siblings as I practised teaching. I later joined St Pauls Theology School. I was given the responsibility to look after reverends' wives and this blocked me from dating," added Mumbi.

She met a man in 1972 who ended up shattering her dreams of getting married after he lied to her.

"Back in 1972, I dated somebody and I knew he had lied to me. I did not change and that is when I made the decision to be single," added Mumbi.

No baby

Mumbi runs a primary school in the heart of Kajiado county after retiring from church service.

The single lady said she has never had a baby and has never regretted it.

"I have never had a baby. I cannot do so due to nature of my job. I cannot sire kids without a husband," she added.

The 60-year-old woman said she dedicated all her life to service and did not think of marriage.

According to her, she chose to remain single after a man she was in love with frustrated her after he failed to show up at their home as agreed for introduction to her mom.
