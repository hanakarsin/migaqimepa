The government has issued travel guidelines for the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) candidates as the national examinations come to an end.

On Friday, April 9, the candidates sat for Biography Paper 3 that commenced at 8am.

A communication from the Office of the Principal Secretary, State Department of Early Learning and Basic Education copied to all county directors education outlined the new guidelines.

The guidelines are for KCSE candidates travelling in and out of Nairobi, Machakos, Kiambu, Kajiado and Nakuru.

"As you are aware, a cessation of movement in and out of the above counties was put in place for purpose of managing the COVID-19 pandemic. However, as the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) comes to an end ,the candidates will need to travel to and out of the area under lockdown," read the statement in part.

The communication signed by Basic Education Principal Secretary Julius Ojwan urged the county directors of education to work with county commissioners and principals of secondary schools to ensure that the candidates are facilitated to travel. 

"Schools should organise with transport companies to have the students picked from schools. Ensure that those travelling are in school uniform and have letters of introduction from the school," Jwan said.

School principals were directed to communicate to the parents of the students on the transport arrangements made. 

"Kindly bring this to the attention of all public and private secondary schools offering KCSE exams," the PS said.

On Friday, March 26, President Uhuru Kenyatta announced stringent measures to help curb the spread of COVID-19 that had escalated since the onset of the third wave in March.

During a televised address to the nation, Uhuru placed the capital Nairobi and four other counties of Kajiado, Machakos, Kiambu, and Nakuru deemed as the virus hotspots under a lockdown.

"There shall be a cessation of all movement by road or rail, or air into and out of the disease infected areas as one zoned area comprising the counties of Nairobi, Kajiado, Machakos, Kiambu, and Nakuru," he said.

The president noted the five counties that account for 70% of the reported COVID-19 cases countrywide, will be placed under lockdown until the current third wave of infections eases.

Curfew hours in the five counties now begin from 8pm to 4am while all in-person gatherings including religious worship have been suspended.

He suspended the ongoing physical learning in universities, colleges and vocational training institutions with the exemption of candidates sitting for final examinations and those in medical training institutions.
