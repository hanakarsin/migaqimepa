How to file KRA returns if not employed or a student

Kenyans love last-minute rush especially when there is a deadline or hefty penalty. For the last few years, KRA pin holders have been flocking various cyber cafes trying to beat the deadline for filing tax returns. Thanks to the KRA iTax system, it is now possible and easy to file nil returns at the comfort of your home. Here is a guide on how to file KRA returns if not employed or you are a student.

Filing returns in the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) website is mandatory for every Kenyan who holds a verified KRA PIN. The government, through KRA, has come up with an online system known as the iTax used by taxpayers to file their returns online. Filing of tax returns applies to the employed, unemployed as well as students who hold the KRA PIN. The taxman also requires the unemployed and the student returns. They, therefore, need to file nil returns since they have no income at all or their income is merely below the taxable amount, which is Kshs.12.000.

How to file KRA returns if not employed may seem like a hectic and time-consuming process, but with the right information, it is an effortless task. Individuals who file nil returns save themselves from the stress of paying the penalty which currently amounts to Kshs.20,000 annually. This penalty falls on those who fail to file their returns. This KRA penalty for not filing returns may hinder you from getting your dream job. This is because most companies nowadays ask for a tax compliance certificate from KRA before giving you a job. With pending penalties, you cannot get this certificate from the taxman.

Do you have to file tax returns if you have no income?

The above is a question that many individuals ask, and the answer is definitely, yes. You will be required to file nil returns in the KRA portal to avoid hefty penalty charges. No need to pay substantial penalties when the whole filling process takes a few minutes. File your returns to be on the safe side.

How to file KRA nil returns for students and unemployed

Below are some simple to follow guidelines on how students should file KRA income tax returns. The same instructions also apply for any unemployed law-abiding Kenyan who want to file KRA returns with no income. You need the following:

  • Your KRA PIN
  • KRA iTax account.

1. Login to the iTax portal

Here you are required to do the following:

  • Search iTax on your phone’s or computer’s browser then click on www.kra.go.ke
  • A window opens up, and on the left-hand side, there is an empty bar in which you are required to fill your KRA PIN or user ID. Then click on the continue button.
  • An empty bar then appears in which you will type in your password accompanied by a security stamp question. The security question resembles a simple mathematical calculation where you are required to type in the answer.
  • Click on login button.

In instances where you forgot your password, the recovery process is simple. Click on forgot password found on the bottom right-hand side of the page. It immediately prompts a small page to open up and help reset your password.

2. Choose to file nil returns

On successful login, a red menu bar appears on your screen. Click on Returns tab, and a drop-down menu appears. From the drop-down menu, click on file nil returns option. A new page pops up where you will fill some information as illustrated below:

  • On the Type, bar choose self
  • On the Taxpayer, pin bar input your KRA PIN
  • On the Tax, obligation bar choose income tax resident

On completion, click on the Next button.

3. Choose income tax resident individual nil e- return

Upon clicking next, a new page appears requiring you to fill in your KRA Pin, wife pin (do not answer if you are not married). Fill also the return period which for the case of KRA nil returns in 2020 will be 01/01/2019 to 31/12/2019, (you may also click a small calendar on the right side which automates the dates).

  • On completion, click on the submit button.
  • If the online submission process was successful, then an e-return acknowledgment receipt appears. Click on download receipt. You may decide to print it or send it to your email for safe storage.

You are all done with filing your nil returns. It's that simple!

KRA new rules for nil returns

Kenya Revenue Authority, through recent technological advancements, has updated its digital platform to help reduce cases of tax evasion. This update will see individuals filing nil returns, having to fill and explicitly declare that they do not get income from any other sources. This declaration will help in tracking down people who evade paying taxes by hiding in the unemployed individuals’ bracket.

Kenyans being well known for the last-minute rush can end up experiencing lots of technical hiccups due to system failure, especially during the last day. These system failures result from thousands of people trying to login to the website and file their returns on the last day. These Kenyans end up blaming KRA saying their systems are faulty and not adequately maintained.
