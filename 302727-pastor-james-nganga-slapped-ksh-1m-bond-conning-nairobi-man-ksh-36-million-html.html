Pastor James Ng'ang'a slapped with KSh 1M bond for conning Nairobi man KSh 3.6 million

- The High Court okayed the prosecution of the controversial pastor for allegedly conning a Nairobi man KSh 3.6 million

- The Nairobi court declined to issue conservatory orders to bar his prosecution

- Ngang'a pleaded not guilty in the case and was subsequently released on a KSh 1 million bond

Controversial Pastor James Ngang’a ‘s woes are far from over after he was charged with obtaining KSh 3.6 million from a tenant over refusal to pay rent arrears for a seven -bedroom house in the leafy suburbs of Karen back in April 6, 2016

Ngang’a appeared before Chief Magistrate Martha Mutuku on Tuesday, April 9, where he was accused of obtaining a sum of KSh 3.6 million from Wickson Njoroge Mwathi by pretending he was in a position to lease him the house on plot no 7792/5 and 779/26 in Karen.

He pleaded not guilty and was freed on a bond of KSh 1 million.

The preacher was represented by lawyer Charles Ongoto.

The controversial televangelist had moved to the High Court on Monday, April 8, in a bid to stop his prosecution but failed.

In his application, Ng'ang'a had argued that the criminal investigations against him and his intended prosecution are based on a commercial transaction which falls under a civil suit.

He wanted the court to stop the Director of Public Prosecution from taking legal actions against him pending the hearing and determination of the petition challenging his intended prosecution.

Ng'ang'a further claimed he had been subjected to untold mental and psychological anguish and torture by the actions of the police who he claimed were hellbent on depriving him of his freedom and security by using different agents and servants based at different police stations.

The pastor added L.R Number 7792/25 and 7792/92 Karen, Nairobi was a residential property which was rented as a seven-bed roomed house.

"One of the conditions was payment of a deposit of two months’ rent which was to be paid to myself, being the landlord of the property which was indeed paid,” his application stated.
