Nairobi by-election: Ferdinand Waititu sues IEBC, EACC

- Ex-Kiambu governor Ferdinand Waititu said his impeachment process is still ongoing and as such, the law allows him to vie for an elective position

- Article 193(3) of the constitution says a person cannot be disqualified from vying for an elective post if he/she still has a pending case challenging their removal

- Chief Justice David Maraga appointed Justice George Dulu, Lady Justice Grace Nzioka and Lady Justice Winfrida Okwany to hear Waititu's petition against his removal

- The IEBC and EACC issued statements suggesting the two commissions were not going to clear Waititu to run in the Nairobi gubernatorial race

- LSK president Nelson Havi said it's only the court that can block a candidate who has been impeached from running in an election

Former Kiambu governor Ferdinand Waititu has sued the Independence Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) and the Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission (EACC) for violation and threatened violation of his rights.

In a petition filed as urgent at the Hight Court, Waititu who has expressed his interest in the Nairobi gubernatorial election accused the two commissions of suggesting they will block his candidature.

The attorney-general was also listed as a respondent in the case that Babayao, as he is also called, asked the court to pronounce itself.

Through his lawyer John Sakwa, Waititu insisted the law allows him to vie for an elective office despite having been impeached by the Senate, citing articles 193 and 99 in the constitution.

The articles allow a person whose integrity has been questioned, for instance, through an impeachment, to run for office as long as they have not exhausted all avenues in challenging their removal.

"A person is not disqualified under clause (2) unless all possibility of appeal or review of the relevant sentence or decision has been exhausted," Article 193(3) of the constitution reads.

Waititu challenged his impeachment at the High Court which means his impeachment process is still underway.

Outgoing Chief Justice David Maraga appointed Justice George Dulu, Lady Justice Grace Nzioka and Lady Justice Winfrida Okwany to hear the petition.

The ex-Kiambu county chief contested his removal stating he was unprocedurally ejected from office.

"The constitutional threshold of two-thirds MCAs was not met in my impeachment. There were only 56 MCAs instead of 62 MCAs out of 92 MCAs total membership of Kiambu county assembly. The Speaker lied to the Senate after receiving a consideration," said Waititiu.

Earlier on, TUKO.co.ke reported that the EACC and IEBC had hinted they may not approve Waititu's candidature since he was removed from office over allegations of abuse of office.

Remarks by the two commissions were echoed by former Senate deputy speaker Kithure Kindiki.

However, according to some legal minds, it is only the courts that can bar an impeached governor from seeking an elective position and not the IEBC or EACC.

"I have seen the statement by EACC that seems to indicate that the commission can make a decision barring anyone from contesting for office. I think that is the height of impunity," Law Society of Kenya President Nelson Havi was quoted by The Standard on Friday, January 1.

The lawyer added IEBC will also be exercising double standards if it may try to bar Waititu from running stating that it set precedence when President Uhuru Kenyatta and his Deputy William Ruto were cleared by the poll agency to vie for office in 2013 yet they were facing charges at The Hague.

A legal tussle between Babayao and the IEBC will be inevitable if his name will be missing in the list.

The ex-Kiambu county chief seeking to run as an independent candidate.

There are about 13 other independent candidates.

Apart from Waititu, deported lawyer Miguna Miguna has expressed interest in the seat through the Third Way Alliance party.

The ODM Party withdrew from the race in favour of Jubilee which has fronted businesswoman Agnes Kagure and former Dagoretti South MP Dennis Waweru.

Waweru and Kagura are expected to lock horns at the primaries which will determine who between them will be the ruling party's flagbearer.
