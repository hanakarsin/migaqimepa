Grade 4, Class 8 and Form Four learners to resume classes on October 12, CS Magoha announces

- Schools will reopen on Monday, October 12, and run for 11 weeks until December 23 which will mark the end of the second term

- Pupils in Grade Four, Class Eight and KCSE candidates in Form Four will be the first to report back to school

- Learners will break for one week and resume classes on January 4, 2021, which will mark the beginning of the third term that will run for 11 weeks

- KCPE examinations will start on March 22-March 24, 2021 while KCSE exams will kick off on March 25-April 16

The Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha has finally announced that primary and secondary schools (public and private) will reopen on Monday, October 12.

National exams

The phased reopening of schools will see Grade Four, Class Eight and Form Four learners report to school first.

The Kenya Certificate of Primary Examinations (KCPE) examinations, Magoha said, will start on March 22, 2021, and end on March 24, 2021.

The Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams will kick off on March 25, 2021, and end on April 16, 2021.

"Although physical distancing will remain A challenge, it should not be used as a bottle neck to keep any child away from school.All teachers are encouraged to continuously provide psychological and spiritual support to learners and school support staff during the duration of the current pandemic," read a statement from the Ministry of Education.

Universities, colleges and TVETs

The directive for the resumption of schooling activities in primary and secondary schools came just a day after universities and tertiary institutions admitted learners in final year classes.

Magoha, however, directed that all colleges and universities must be linked to a health facility before reopening.

The institutions, Magoha said, are required to enforce strict adherence to COVID-19 health and safety protocols which include monitoring of body temperature for all learners and all other people accessing the institutions.

More confusion

The school reopening order by CS Magoha, nevertheless, strewn more confusion on whether or not primary and secondary schools have achieved the required level of preparedness to deal with the new disease.

On Monday, September 28, when President Uhuru Kenyatta eased restrictions, the head of state said that schools were only going to be opened when the safety of learners could be fully guaranteed.

"As we progressively de-escalate the containment measures and resume a sense of normalcy on education, our paramount consideration both as a government and as parents is the safety and the well-being of our children. The lives and health of our children is not a matter of debate. Learning institutions should be reopened only when we can sufficiently guarantee the safety of our children," said Uhuru.

Not in good financial shape

Following the Tuesday, October 6, order by the CS section of parents has asked the government to give them more time to ready their children for school, terming the order as "short notice".

Some of them stated that they were not in good financial shape since most of their income streams had been choked by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Others broke into jubilation claiming they were eager to send their children back to school.
