Gospel artiste and presidential aspirant Reuben Kigame has declared he will fight corruption the way he has rebuked false prophets.

In April, the singer announced that he would be going for the top seat, having experienced what he said was "bad governance" by the current regime.

“As an apologist, I have called out by name, corrected and rebuked false prophets and teachers and sought to protect faithful and citizens of the world against financial and spiritual manipulation despite Great opposition and danger. Believe me; I can deal with corruption,'' Kigame said.

Rebuked false prophets and teachers

Kigame said that as an apologist, he had called out by name, corrected and rebuked false prophets and teachers and sought to protect faithful and citizens of the world against financial and spiritual manipulation despite great opposition and danger.

''Believe me, I can deal with corruption,'' Kigame said.

On Thursday, July 29, the Federal Party of Kenya (FPK) has settled on Kigame as its flagbearer in the 2022 General Election.

He had previously disclosed he would vie for the presidency as an independent candidate.

Kigame is among politicians seeking to succeed Uhuru

Kigame has joined the growing list of politicians seeking to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta when his second and last term ends in 2022.

Taking to his official Twitter handle, Kigame said that the move signalled a new dawn for the country.

''A million thanks to the Federal Party of Kenya for the decision to support me to become the fifth president of Kenya. It is not a light matter to my team and me! The time for inclusive politics and the birth of a better Kenya is here,'' Kigame wrote.

TUKO.co.ke understands FPK nominated 20 National Assembly candidates for the 2007 General Election, receiving 0.2% of the vote and failing to win a seat.

In the 2013 General Election, the party nominated 56 National Assembly candidates, increasing its vote share to 1.6% and winning three seats; Michael Aringo Onyura (Betula), Peter Safari Shehe (Ganze) and Charles Gimose (Hamisi).

The political outfit also won one seat in the Senate, Ali Abdi Bule in Tana River county.

Political activism

Known for his activism, Kigame has been at the forefront of calling out the Jubilee government mishandling the country.

Kigame insisted that Kenya was in a bad place, and it thus was his obligation to deal with the rot in government or else future generations would blame him.
