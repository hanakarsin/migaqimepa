William Ruto Shares Letter to Bishops Inviting Them to Reunite Him with Uhuru

  • William Ruto noted that he was ready to make peace with Uhuru and see the country move forward as per the call by Catholic bishops
  • Ruto shared the letter to rubbish claims that he had snubbed church call for reconciliation talks after the Catholic bishops claimed neither Ruto nor Uhuru had reverted
  • The bishops had offered to mediate and reconcile President Uhuru and Ruto, stating that their frosty relationship was hurting Kenyans

Deputy President William Ruto has shared a response letter to bishops after they offered to reunite him with President Uhuru Kenyatta and put an end to their frosty relationship.

In the letter dated September 16, Ruto noted that he was ready to make peace with Uhuru and see the country move forward as per the call by the clerics.

Ruto's response to bishops

"Greetings in the name of Christ, our Lord. Your statement of 15 September 2021 a call to keep hope alive is a most welcome development. I am aware that as a leader and deputy president, therefore, I register my agreement with your message and the exhortation therein," he wrote.

Ruto's letter is contrary to a report filed by a section of local media houses that the DP had Uhuru had snubbed church call for reconciliation talks.

In a report filed by the Sunday Nation, the Catholics bishops stated that the two leaders had not responded to their invitation to peace talks.

However, Ruto stated he had responded to the invitation saying the country was faced with challenges that threatened the country's sustainability; thus, reconciling with his boss was paramount for the country's peace.

"Kenya faces challenges, a number of which threaten the welfare of individuals as well as the sustainability of national cohesion and stability. It is our responsibility to marshal all efforts to solve these problems and to ensure that the government policy honors the freedom, dignity, and humanity of Kenyans in word and deed," he stated.

Nyeri archdiocese head Anthony Muheria said that the innovation was still on, and they were waiting for the two leaders to respond.

"The invitation still stands. Even today, we pray for the invitation to become a reality, Muheria told Sunday Nation.

Uhuru and Ruto have not been seeing eye to eye of late, with their fall-out playing out in their public utterances, in decisions of their respective allies, and actions of state officers.

First response from Ruto

Last month TUKO.co.ke reported that Ruto had welcomed the move by Catholic bishops to reconcile him with President Uhuru.

Speaking on Thursday, September 16, the deputy president said he was ready to reconcile with his boss without any conditions adding that he respects the president as his superior.
