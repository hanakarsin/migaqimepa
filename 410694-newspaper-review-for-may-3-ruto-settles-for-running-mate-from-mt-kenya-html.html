Newspapers Review For May 3: Ruto Settles For Running Mate From Mt Kenya

The dailies extensively talked about the 2022 politics, including the support promised to Deputy President William Ruto by a section of Mt Kenya politicians.

Ruto promised to draw his running mate from the region to return the favour accorded to him.

The papers also touched on the division that has rocked the ODM party regarding the changes to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) document.

1. People Daily

The publication covered the support promised to Ruto by a section of Mt Kenya legislators who he met in Maasai Mara to discuss the plan of succeeding Uhuru.

The legislators declared to have endorsed the DP, stating they would be embarking on selling his name in the region.

Even as they vowed to back the DP, they were categorical that he gives them other slots, including the running mate, among other benefits.

Among those touted as favourites to deputise Ruto include former Agriculture CS Mwangi Kiunjuri, Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagua, and Tharaka Nithi senator Kithure Kindiki.

2. The Standard

The paper looked at the governing model that Ruto intends to employ if he is sworn in as Kenya's fifth president.

The daily delved deeper into the bottom-up economics; an approach vouched for by the DP.

He said he would dwell much on the unemployed, the poor and the population in the rural settings.

Ruto promised to focus much on enabling an environment that would reward hard work and give life to creativity.

His key agenda regarding the particular approach is to empower small businesses and to increase earnings to farmers.

This all comes up as critics pounce on Ruto for not implementing the model with his boss.

3. Taifa Leo

The publication reported on the divisions that are being witnessed in the ODM party regarding the BBI bill.

The bone of contention happens to be the extra 70 constituencies proposed in the bill, a faction led by Siaya senator James Orengo disputing it.

The other divide in the party, led by John Mbadi and Junet Mohamed, maintain the constituencies are necessary, and thus the bill should be passed without any changes made to it.

The division has led to public brawls among the parties, the John Mbadi led team accusing their counterparts, led by James Orengo and Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo, of being traitorous and working in cahoots to succeed Raila Odinga as the Nyanza region political chief.

4. The Star

The daily reported about the dispute pitting two major universities and the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA).

The University of Nairobi (UoN) and Kenyatta University (KU) were reported to have had their accounts frozen over taxes.

According to the taxman, the two institutions have not remitted their statutory deductions for years, accumulating billions of shillings.
