I am campaigning while Raila is busy holding press conferences- Uhuru

- President Uhuru Kenyatta has asked NASA leader Raila Odinga to quit issuing unnecessary demands ahead of the repeat election

- He castigated the Opposition for wasting time holding press conferences instead of campaigning

- The President claimed the opposition is not ready for the election compared to Jubilee leaders who are bus holding campaigns in readiness for the repeat election

President Uhuru Kenyatta has said Kenyans are ready for election and the Opposition should stop issuing unnecessary demands.

Uhuru said unlike the Opposition which keeps on addressing press conferences in Nairobi, Jubilee Party is already campaigning in preparations for the October 17 repeat election.

“We have already hit the ground while our colleagues in the Opposition are still wasting their energies issuing statements in press conferences in Nairobi. We are ready for the poll. This is why we have embarked on campaigns to woo support from Kenyans,”said the Jubilee party presidential candidate.

The President, who was addressing Nakuru residents at Free Area on Wednesday, September 13 castigated NASA leaders for continuously antagonizing Kenyans who want to move on with their day-to-day activities, saying elections should not derail the development of the country.

“Kenyans want to do away with issues of election. They want to carry on with their day-to-day activities away from political life and that’s why they are preparing for the October 17 polls,” he said.

Deputy President William Ruto said the Opposition has nothing new to offer Kenyans and has already sensed defeat.

“They know we will beat them like the way we did on August 8. This is why they are now claiming that they will not participate in the October poll,” said Ruto.
