Masinde Muliro professor talks of finding witchcraft paraphernalia at his home

- It appears that witchcraft is a dreaded practice in Kenya even a university professor at Masinde Muliro had to report fears of his life at Kakamega police station

A university professor in Kakamega is living in fear after he found witchcraft paraphernalia at his home.

In a statement that Stephen Odebero has recorded with the police, the don claims that his life is in danger and that walking home to find witchcraft paraphernalia was the embodiment of a death threat.

According to his statement, the witchcraft stock includes a live hen with no feathers, the head of a cat, what looked like a cat's feet in a black paper bag, male and female undergarments and a red rope used to tag a hearse.

Odebero, who is also an education boss in the area claims that the motive to kill him could be linked to his recent decision to close down institutions.

It appears leaving witchcraft paraphernalia at homes and offices to send a message is a common practice in the Western part of Kenya.

Recently, dried frogs, animal bones, roots and ashes send MCAs at the Vihiga county fleeing for their lives after they found the stock in their assembly under the clerk's chair.

Radio Jambo reported that the county's sergeant-at-arms George Kibisu claimed that the witchcraft stuff was so powerful that he actually felt dizzy.

Josephat Musambayi was the clerk who had been suspended after claims of corruption were leveled against him.
