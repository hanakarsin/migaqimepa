If you want to demolish, go ahead, I dont care - Taj Mall owner

- Ramesh Gorasia said he was ready to sell the property to government for public use instead of demolishing it

- His statement came after government issued a vacation notice to Taj Mall tenants

- Gorasia was ordered to demolish the multi-million shilling building by August 30 or the government does so at his cost

- Taj Mall is said to be standing on a road reserve and has been blamed for poor design of Outering Road

The owner of the multi-million imposing Air Gate Mall formerly Taj Mall is now prepared to face off with the government in court as demolition of his property nears.

Speaking to the press on Thursday, August 16, Ramesh Gorasia said the government was frustrating investors in the country through the demolitions.

"If you want to demolish the building, go ahead, I don't care. But If you have to demolish come and demolish it completely, don't come and demolish it partly, if you don't have money to clear it off please don't try to demolish it," said Gorasia

The businessman said he was ready to face government in court once the building is destroyed and seek compensation for the losses accumulated from the demolition as he has all the legal documents for his property.

However, he offered to give authorities a better deal of negotiating with him to sell the building to the government for public use instead of destroying it.

Gorasia's statement came hours after the government through the Multi-Sectoral Committee on Unsafe Structures issued a public notice to Taj Mall tenants asking them to vacate the building by August 30, 2018.

"Upon the expiry of this notice, the illegal development will be demolished or removed from the road reserve and wayleave at the owner (s)/ developer (s) risk and cost," read the notice.

There have been demolitions in the recent past days with government aiming at reclaiming grabbed public land and riparian areas.

The public was waiting with bated breath to see if the mall would go down as it stands on a road reserve.

Taj Mall has been blamed for the poor design of Outering Road and past efforts to have it knocked down have failed.
