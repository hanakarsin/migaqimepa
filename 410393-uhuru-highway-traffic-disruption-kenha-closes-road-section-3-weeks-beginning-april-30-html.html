Uhuru Highway Traffic Disruption: KeNHA Closes Road Section for 3 More Weeks

- Kenya National Highway Authority (KeNHA) said there would be traffic disruptions along the highway between Bunyala and Nyayo roundabouts

- The closure will be effective Friday, April 30, and end on May 30 to allow for the ongoing construction of the Nairobi Expressway on Mombasa Road

- In January 2021, Transport Cabinet Secretary James Macharia gazetted the expressway as a toll road once completed in 2022

Motorists and other road users plying Uhuru Highway in Nairobi will be forced to find alternative routes for the next 21 days.

In a statement on Thursday, April 29, the Kenya National Highway Authority (KeNHA) said there would be traffic disruptions along the highway between Bunyala and Nyayo roundabouts.

The KeNHA director-general Peter Mundinia said the closure would be effective Friday, April 30, and end on May 30 to allow for the ongoing construction of the Nairobi Expressway on Mombasa Road.

In the meantime, motorists heading from town will divert at Bunyala roundabout, cutting Bunyala Road onto Dunga before returning to Mombasa Road using Lusaka Road.

Those driving to town through Mombasa road will take a detour at Nyayo stadium roundabout into Aerodrome road before linking up at the Bunyala roundabout into the main highway.

Multibillion project

The 18.5km road starts at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and ends at the James Gichuru junction along Waiyaki Way in Westlands.

The construction of the double-decker highway was initially set to begin in August 2019 but was pushed to October due to financial-related difficulties.

The gazettement means motorists will pay a fee to use the road, which will be completed in 2022.

“In exercise of the powers conferred by section 3 (2A) of the Public Roads Toll Act, (Cap 407), the dual carriageway with Class A standard that connects the Mlolongo with James Gichuru Road along the median strip of the A8 National Road are declared to be Toll Roads with immediate effect,” the notice read in part.
